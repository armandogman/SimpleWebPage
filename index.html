The cost of implementing cybersecurity systems that use threat analysis can vary depending on the company's size, the system's complexity, and the level of protection required. One of the pillars of sustainability is the economic impact of cybersecurity systems. The initial investment, Implementing a cybersecurity system that uses threat analysis requires an initial investment in hardware, software, and personnel. This can include the cost of purchasing and installing security tools such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and antivirus software.

Ongoing maintenance, Maintaining a cybersecurity system that uses threat analysis requires ongoing expenses for updating software, monitoring security events, and managing security incidents. This can include the cost of hiring dedicated security personnel or outsourcing to a managed security service provider (MSSP). Training is another cost to ensuring that employees are trained on the proper use of security tools and protocols can be a significant cost for companies. This can include the cost of hiring trainers, developing training materials, and providing ongoing training to employees. Regulatory compliance with industry-specific regulations such as HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and GDPR can require significant investments in cybersecurity systems and processes. Failure to comply to federal laws can result in fines and reputational damage. Cybersecurity incidents can cause significant business interruption, leading to lost revenue and productivity if a data breach has not been discovered. Companies may need to invest in business continuity and disaster recovery planning to mitigate the impact of these incidents. Reputation damage could be a significant financial loss for businesses. Companies may need to invest in public relations and communication efforts to manage the fallout from these incidents.
