Introduction

Cybersecurity threat analysts are professionals who work to identify and mitigate threats to computer systems and networks. They use a variety of techniques, tools, and technologies to identify vulnerabilities, monitor network activity, and respond to incidents. Cybersecurity threat analysts are crucial in protecting organizations and individuals from cyber threats such as malware, ransomware, phishing attacks, and other types of cybercrime. The impact of cyber threats on society, the economy, and the environment cannot be overstated. Cybersecurity incidents can cause a wide range of negative effects, including financial losses, data breaches, identity theft, and even physical harm. In addition, cyber attacks can disrupt critical infrastructure, such as power grids, transportation systems, and healthcare networks, leading to widespread chaos and loss of life.
