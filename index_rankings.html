Economic (ranked 2nd with a weighted total of 8.2)

  • The initial investment and ongoing maintenance of cybersecurity systems can be expensive, but it is essential to protect the company's assets and reputation. Failure to comply with regulations or respond adequately to a cyber attack can result in fines, lost revenue, and productivity.

  • Reputation damage could be a significant financial loss for businesses. Implementing a cybersecurity system that uses threat analysis can be expensive, but the costs of a cyber attack can be even more significant. Companies must weigh the costs and benefits of cybersecurity investments and make informed decisions.

Social Ranked (1st with a weighted total of 9.2)

  • Cybersecurity threats are not only technical but also social. Many of the threats are made using social engineering techniques that can grant access to sensitive and private systems and data. Humans' behavior and actions can significantly impact the security of any computer system.

  • Cyber-attacks have social implications, such as the reputation of an organization being ruined. When someone with a major following gets hacked, that attacker has access to possibly millions of people's sensitive information.

  • Social media platforms can be used to commit identity theft and distribute malware or harmful links to gain access to other accounts.

Social Ranked (3rd with a weighted total of 3.8)

  • Cybersecurity is crucial for environmental sustainability as ethical hacking can prevent attacks on critical infrastructure such as power grids, which can lead to environmental disasters like the release of pollutants.

  • Power outages caused by cyber-attacks can disrupt critical infrastructure and impact the environment, as well as the homes of the citizens.

  • Utilities should take a proactive approach to cybersecurity, including regular security assessments and drills, as well as investment in new technologies to detect and prevent cyberattacks, to protect the environment and the people.
